## Overview

KRAS4B is the canonical 188-amino-acid KRAS isoform and functions as a small GTPase that cycles between GDP-bound inactive and GTP-bound active states. Its oncogenic behavior is driven by hotspot mutations that disrupt normal nucleotide cycling, alter hydrolysis or exchange, and sustain downstream signaling.[1][2]

This atlas was designed as a compact learning and reference resource for students, researchers, and translational scientists who want to understand KRAS4B at the codon, nucleotide, structural, and mechanistic levels.

## What this atlas shows

- KRAS4B protein architecture, including the G-domain, P-loop, Switch I, Switch II, hypervariable region (HVR), polybasic region, and CaaX motif.
- Mutation hotspot distribution across the KRAS4B sequence.
- A complete codon atlas from 1–188.
- Expanded interpretation of major hotspot codons: G12, G13, Q61, K117, and A146.
- Genotype-to-phenotype logic: nucleotide change → amino-acid substitution → structural change → biochemical defect → signaling outcome.
- GDP/GTP molecular switch regulation through GEFs and GAPs.
- KRAS4B-specific membrane localization logic driven by the HVR and post-translational processing.
- Clinical and translational context for major KRAS hotspot classes.

## Key scientific ideas behind the atlas

KRAS mutations are not randomly distributed. The most common activating mutations cluster around codons 12, 13, 61, 117, and 146, which are positioned around the nucleotide-binding and catalytic machinery.[2][3]

Codon-level interpretation matters. A “KRAS mutation” is not a sufficient biological description, because different codons and even different substitutions within the same codon can produce distinct biochemical and clinical behavior.[2][4]

KRAS4B biology also depends on membrane targeting. Its C-terminal hypervariable region and CaaX motif undergo post-translational processing that supports plasma membrane association and proper signaling output.[1][5]

## Why this project exists

Most KRAS figures either focus only on hotspot frequency, only on structure, or only on therapy. This project was built to combine all major layers of KRAS4B interpretation into a single visual system that helps users learn:

- where mutations occur,
- why they matter structurally,
- how they alter KRAS switching behavior,
- how signaling consequences emerge,
- and why variant-level interpretation is necessary.

## Intended audience

- Students learning cancer biology, structural biology, or molecular oncology.
- Researchers studying KRAS mutation classes and signaling behavior.
- Computational scientists working in CADD, structural bioinformatics, or mutation interpretation.
- Translational users who need a compact conceptual map of KRAS4B variant biology.

## Main hotspot codons covered

- G12
- G13
- Q61
- K117
- A146

These codons were prioritized because they capture the dominant activating and mechanistically important mutation classes in KRAS.[2][3]

## How to read the atlas

1. Start with the codon.
2. Check the wild-type DNA triplet and mutant substitution.
3. Map the variant to its structural region.
4. Infer the likely biochemical defect.
5. Connect that defect to signaling and translational relevance.

## Project status

This repository currently contains the visual atlas and supporting presentation text. Future extensions may include cleaner reference annotation, variant-specific expansion panels, and companion educational notes.
